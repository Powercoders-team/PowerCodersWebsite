HTML for Beginners | Module One: Basics of HTML

Before we dive into coding, let's take a look at what elements are used in HTML.

Headings and Paragraphs

Common text types in HTML are headings and paragraphs.

There are six different types of headings, classified as <h1>, <h2>, <h3>, <h4>, <h5> and <h6>.

As the numbers - or values - next to the h get bigger, the size of the heading gets smaller.

Headings are used when the programmer wants to define a title.

Paragraphs have a <p> tag and are this size.

Paragraphs are regular content on a webpage, like this text!

Opening and Closing Tags

Headings and paragraphs are not self-closing tags, which means you need to open them and close them.

Opening tags have the name of the element wrapped in a <>

Example: <h1>

Closing tags look just like opening tags, except they have a / before the name.

Example: </h1>
